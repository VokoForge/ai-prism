## III. Five Core Highlights That Will Keep You Up at Night

### Highlight 1: Everything in the Terminal

OpenClaw's design philosophy is brutally simple -- **the terminal is everything**.

No more opening browsers, no more switching windows, no more copy-pasting. Just speak plain English in your terminal, and it gets the job done. For example, say: "Find any memory leak code in this project," and it immediately starts scanning, analyzing, and generating a report. All you have to do is sit back and sip your coffee.

This is a complete reconstruction of the developer workflow. We used to be accustomed to the "editor for code, browser for docs, terminal for commands" trifecta. OpenClaw merges all three into one, letting you complete every operation within a single interface. What you save is not time -- it is the cost of context switching. And attention is the most scarce resource a programmer has.

### Highlight 2: It Actually Works, Not Just Talks

This is the most fundamental difference between OpenClaw and every other AI chat tool on the market.

It can directly read and write files, execute commands, manage processes, and operate databases. Your machine is its hands and feet; you give the order, and it executes. It is like hiring a master craftsman who can both design and swing the hammer, rather than a "keyboard warrior" who just stands there watching.

Here is an example: tell it to "convert all callback functions in the project to async," and it will not just give you a list of suggestions. It will open the files, locate the code, modify it, save it, run the tests, and confirm everything passes. The entire process unfolds on your screen, and you just nod to confirm occasionally. This is not an assistant -- this is an indefatigable "coding partner."

### Highlight 3: Jensen Huang Personally Endorsed It

At GTC 2026, Jensen Huang dropped a bombshell that electrified the entire audience:

**"OpenClaw is the next Linux."**

How heavy do those words land? Consider this: Linux is the foundational infrastructure of the entire internet. It powers the vast majority of servers, supercomputers, and Android devices worldwide. By comparing OpenClaw to Linux, Huang made his point crystal clear -- he believes OpenClaw will become an entirely new underlying ecosystem, just as Linux did, reshaping the entire developer world.

And Huang was not just talking. NVIDIA subsequently announced a dedicated compute support plan for OpenClaw, allowing developers to use high-performance GPUs to run OpenClaw for free. This move is a clear bet on the next era.

### Highlight 4: The Founder Got Poached

Here comes the most dramatic twist. OpenClaw's founder was personally poached by Sam Altman.

The story reads like a movie script: an independent open-source project goes global in two months, and then the head of Silicon Valley's most powerful AI company personally shows up with an offer. What does that tell you? It tells you OpenClaw is not one of those "looks cool, works meh" vanity projects. It is a fundamental shift that genuinely threatens the lifelines of industry giants.

After the founder joined, speculation ran rampant about deeper integration and a massive influx of resources. Yet OpenClaw, as an open-source project, continues to thrive in the community, showing zero signs of being "gutted from within."

### Highlight 5: Blazing Performance

Built with Rust, the core engine is so fast you barely feel any latency. The moment you hit Enter, it starts executing. Many users reported: "The first time I used it, I thought I had forgotten to press Enter."

Compared to browser-based AI tools that take forever to load every single time, OpenClaw is like upgrading from a bicycle straight to a jet aircraft. Startup time is under 0.3 seconds, memory footprint is just a few dozen megabytes, and it runs like the wind even on a ten-year-old laptop. This "lightweight and efficient" DNA is cut from the same cloth as the Linux kernel philosophy.

## V. Quick Start in Three Steps

If you want to try it right now, it only takes three steps.

**Step 1: Install**

Open your terminal and install it with your favorite package manager. One command, fully automatic. No complex dependencies, no annoying environment setup. Ready to use right after installation.

**Step 2: Configure the Model**

OpenClaw supports virtually every major large language model on the market. You can specify which model to use in the config file, whether it is a flagship cloud model or a locally deployed open-source model. Want to save money? Plug in a local model. Want top performance? Plug in a cloud flagship. Your call.

**Step 3: Start Chatting**

Just type your request directly in the terminal. No special syntax to learn. You speak human, it does human work. For example: "List all code files in this directory with more than 100 lines," "Add type annotations to these functions," or "Run the tests and tell me if anything fails." Every instruction is executed with the diligence of a senior engineer on your team.
